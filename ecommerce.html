Scalable eCommerce Database Architecture (MS SQL)

Project Overview: The project focuses on creating a robust and efficient SQL database to support E-commerce or online store operations. The primary goal is to ensure seamless transaction processing, effective inventory management, and insightful analytics capabilities.

Each table was meticulously crafted to maintain data integrity and optimise query performance.

Customer: Customers' details are stored with a unique ID, facilitating personalised interactions and seamless tracking.

Products: Information about available products is organised systematically, encompassing product ID, name, description, price, and stock quantity.

Category: Product categories and their descriptions are meticulously catalogued, enhancing navigation and organisation.

Subcategory: Subcategories further refine product classification, facilitating granular browsing and search functionalities.

The cart entity facilitates customers' temporary storage of selected items, streamlining the purchasing process.

Order: Comprehensive order details are captured within this entity, ensuring accuracy and transparency throughout the transaction lifecycle.

Order Details: Detailed information on individual orders is meticulously documented, providing insights into purchase history and preferences.

Payments: Payment-related data is securely managed within this entity, ensuring seamless and reliable transaction processing.

Shipping: Shipping details are meticulously recorded, facilitating efficient order fulfilment and delivery logistics.

Stored Function for Total Calculation: A vital feature of this project is the development of a stored function that calculates the total cost of an order based on the quantity and unit price of items. This function efficiently computes the total and ensures accurate billing for customers.

Stored Procedure for High-Value Orders: A stored procedure was created to identify high-value orders exceeding a certain threshold. This procedure utilises the stored function to calculate order totals, enabling automated detection of significant orders.

Triggers: Within the database structure, I implemented triggers to automate the management of the order status and stock quantities based on order activity. When an order is placed and payment confirmed, the trigger dynamically adjusts the stock quantities, deducting the amount purchased from the respective product's stock.

Views: I integrated several tables through joins to establish a comprehensive view dedicated to reporting and sales order management.

Scalability and Flexibility: The database design was not just a solution for the present but a future-proof investment. It was carefully crafted to accommodate future scalability requirements and adapt to evolving business needs. The platform can seamlessly grow and develop by maintaining a modular and extensible architecture.
